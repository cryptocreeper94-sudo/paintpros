Local Hardware Partnerships vs. Sherwin‑Williams: Why This Matters for a $2M Painting Company

Purpose of This Page
This page explains — in clear, conversational terms — why a $2M/year painting company gains far more marketing value from partnering with local hardware stores (Ace, True Value, mom‑and‑pop shops) than from relying solely on Sherwin‑Williams or Benjamin Moore. It includes realistic numbers based on our actual paint usage and shows the opportunity we’re currently missing.

---

1. What Our Paint Spend Actually Looks Like
Industry data shows that paint and materials typically make up 20–30% of total project cost for residential painting.  
This is consistent across multiple industry sources.

For a $2,000,000/year painting company, that means:

Estimated annual paint/material spend:
$400,000 – $600,000 per year

This is the real amount Sherwin‑Williams sees from us — not $2M.

---

2. What Sherwin‑Williams Provides at a $400k–$600k Spend
Sherwin‑Williams is a $23.57 billion company. Even a $600k account is a tiny fraction of their total revenue.

Typical benefits at this level:
- PRO+ volume pricing (usually 15–30% off depending on product)  
- Occasional 40% off public sales (not exclusive)  
- Free delivery  
- Credit terms  
- A rep who manages many accounts  
- Access to pro-only products  

These are operational conveniences, not growth drivers.

Sherwin‑Williams does not provide:
- Marketing exposure  
- Community visibility  
- Local referrals  
- Brand trust transfer  

They help us operate — but they don’t help us grow.

---

3. What We’re Missing by Not Partnering With Local Hardware Stores
Local hardware stores operate on trust, relationships, and community presence.  
They see homeowners before they choose a painter.

Missed opportunities include:
- In-store referrals (“Call these guys — they’re who we trust.”)  
- Business card / brochure placement  
- DIY-to-Pro conversions (customers who walk in planning to paint but change their mind)  
- Co-marketing events (color workshops, seasonal promotions, Q&A days)  
- Local trust transfer — the store vouches for us  
- Visibility in a place homeowners already trust  

These stores are community hubs. Their recommendation carries more weight than any discount Sherwin‑Williams can offer.

---

4. The Numbers That Actually Matter
Even conservative estimates show how powerful these partnerships can be.

If two local hardware stores send:
- 5 referrals per month each → 10 total  
- With a 40% close rate (hardware referrals close higher due to trust)  
- At an average job value of $3,000–$6,000  

That equals:
- 4 new jobs per month  
- $12,000–$24,000/month in revenue  
- $144,000–$288,000/year  

That’s 7–14% of our entire annual revenue — from just two stores.

Sherwin‑Williams cannot generate this kind of marketing lift.

---

5. The Trust Factor (The Real Multiplier)
When a homeowner is standing in a local hardware store, they’re talking to someone they trust.

If that person says:

> “These are the painters we recommend — they do great work.”

That referral is almost guaranteed.

Local stores protect their reputation.  
They won’t recommend someone unless they believe in them.  
That trust transfers directly to us.

---

6. The Simple Comparison

Sherwin‑Williams Gives Us:
- Discounts  
- Delivery  
- Credit terms  
- Product consistency  

Local Hardware Stores Give Us:
- Warm referrals  
- Community trust  
- Visibility  
- Lead flow  
- Brand presence  

One saves money.  
The other makes money.

---

7. The Line to Use in the Meeting
> “Sherwin‑Williams helps us operate. Local hardware stores help us grow.”
